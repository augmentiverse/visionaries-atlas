Scoring methodology

Evidence-informed, not official rankings.

The profile scores are editorial indicators based on documented achievements: shipped products, technical authorship, company scale, adoption, market creation, cultural influence, and long-term institutional impact. No public database provides verified scores for vision, design, execution, or impact, so every number should be read as a transparent research aid rather than a factual measurement.

Research notes

How the portal treats evidence.

Visionary Atlas is designed for public learning without flattening history into hero worship. A figure is included when their work produced a durable shift in scientific method, technology, civic life, design culture, or the public imagination. Each summary separates biographical identity from contribution, then points readers to an institutional or community-maintained record for verification.

The current profile facts combine the supplied archive data with a source-first editorial model. For production use, each profile should keep visible citations to Wikidata or Wikipedia and, where available, institution-level sources such as company biographies, Nobel Prize records, NASA, Stanford Encyclopedia entries, or official foundation pages.
